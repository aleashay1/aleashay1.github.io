Waves

How do I state simply,
That I am breaking trying
To feed what it is inside of you
That I wish to keep.
It was never mine to begin with.
I don't want you to leave,
Leave like everyone else has,
So I break.
I take it upon myself to
Board the ship, setting sail to
The land of your groin,
Weary and exhausted.

I man the ship, all parts,
To all ports.
It is not that I don't love being captain,
It is that I am manning it alone.
I pull the strings,
To make you feel the wind,
Let the blazen wisps of desire
Strike you like the back of a hand
To the face,
Steer the wheel to the left
To cultivate the waves
That make you weak and
Wanting more,
And steer right to
Show you the wave is something even I, too, can coast.
I stand on the lookout post,
Seeking more waves to drift into
Whenever the opportunity appears,
To show you that you are wanted
Even when I am exhausted.
To show you I am always here,
Trying to ride the ship
However long you wish.
I desperately run end to end,
Bow to stern,
Making ends meet,
Just to please you.
In that ship,

There is all the room to run
Aimlessly, twirl around and dance,
Yet I have no energy to
Frolic about while I wait
For you to take the lead.
Wondering when you'll
Notice all the work I've done.
The only dancing is the sacred escapade
Of dodging the holes in the floor,
So I don't fall into the bottom,
Hit the floor of fear,
And deteriorate in the darkness
That this ship will just not do it for you,
And that I can not trust that
When the time comes, and the decision
Is laid in front of you, that you will board the ship,
Not stay on port.
Rather than wait for that time to come,
I reign the ship widely through
The stormy waters,
Waiting for you to take the strings,
So I can feel the wisps,
To let the flag fly so
I can feel the caressing.
I have apparently left no room in an empty ship
For you to show me
The rails,
While I'm barely grasping the mast.
The ship is empty.
You are nowhere to be found.
And all I wanted was for you
To show up and sail me.
