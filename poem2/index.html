Mouthwash

I spent ages trying to fill my mouth
With a sweet taste I dreamt about at night.
And once I got that taste, I didn't dare take it for granted.
I let it inhabit my taste buds,
Crawl under my tongue and into the deep blue veins streaming straight to my heart.
I swished with it every time I got the chance, like I was addicted.
I let it flow into my body.
I let it overtake me.
I held onto it, and I didn't plan on letting go.
Overtime, I soon found myself doing all I could to clear my spirit of this taste.
It became foul.
It lost it's luster.
I've spent many nights trying to rid the taste from my mouth,
It making me sick and nauseous.
I never had been able to get rid of it myself.

A new taste has flooded my mouth.
Swarming in just like the last time,
But this one delighted me, for it was gentle.
Gentle, caressing, and it slowly slid into my taste buds,
My veins,
My being.
It tenderly and swiftly darted around, sneaky almost.
It's benevolence took me over.
I again will not take it for granted,
And make sure that this
Sugary sweet, addicting taste
Stays so flavorful, for as long
As this being walks the earth.
