Come Home

Bubbles

And I think it is normal,
I think it is normal that
On first experiences
We have doubt.
I don't think we are
Supposed to be perfectly made,
Solidified with clay,
Molded to glassy perfection
In the kiln and set out
For display on the
First attempt at something new.
Wrapped up in a tight little bubble,
The vision is clear
And the world is big,
And everything is so...

Pop.

A new experience.
The bubble has been
Burst wide open.
The world flushes in,
Drowning the insides of the bubble.
A whiplash of an experience,
Questioning the totality of
All identites the bubble is made up of,
As the new experience rapes the bubble's insides.
There is no more drifting.
No more floating,
Just falling and then...

Splat.

Hitting the ground running,
The insides of the bubble
Grasps for any understanding
Of something that it knows.
It's entire world has been shattered.
It doesn't know what to do
With itself in this new terrain.
Breathless.
The uncontained insides of the bubble
Loses sight on what to do,
Other than to wave the white flag,
And surrender to the contemporary.
